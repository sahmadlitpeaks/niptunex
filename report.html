Inside the report

Clear, structured, counselling-ready

Every NIPTune X report is organised into three clearly labelled sections — each with a plain-language summary for parents and a clinical detail page for the physician.

Section 1 · Baby

Fetal chromosome screening

Cell-free DNA from the placenta lets us screen for the most common chromosome conditions — without any risk to your pregnancy.

  • Trisomy 21 (Down syndrome)An extra copy of chromosome 21.
  • Trisomy 18 (Edwards syndrome)An extra copy of chromosome 18.
  • Trisomy 13 (Patau syndrome)An extra copy of chromosome 13.
  • Optional add-onsFetal sex and sex chromosome aneuploidies (Turner, Klinefelter, XXX, XYY).

Trisomy 21

Low risk · 1 in 10,000

Trisomy 18

Low risk · < 1 in 10,000

Trisomy 13

Low risk · < 1 in 10,000

Sample report card

Each result is presented with a clear classification, the underlying numerical risk, and a plain-language explanation of what the value means.

Section 2 · Mother

Maternal genetic predispositions

Genetic markers associated with conditions that can affect the course of pregnancy — so your clinician can monitor you proactively.

  • Gestational diabetesRisk variants influencing glucose metabolism during pregnancy.
  • Preeclampsia & hypertensionVariants linked to blood pressure regulation and placental health.
  • Recurrent miscarriage markersIncluding inherited thrombophilia variants such as Factor V Leiden.
  • Personalised guidanceEach finding is paired with suggested monitoring or lifestyle considerations.

What this means for you

Predisposition is not destiny. These insights help your care team tailor monitoring frequency, lifestyle conversations, and — when needed — early interventions.

Section 3 · Nutrition

Personalised micronutrient profile

Genetic variants that influence how your body absorbs and uses key nutrients — supporting smarter, more personalised supplementation during pregnancy.

  • Iron metabolismAbsorption efficiency and risk of deficiency anaemia.
  • Folate & B-vitaminsMTHFR-style variants relevant to folic acid recommendations.
  • Vitamin C, D, magnesiumPersonalised guidance for everyday wellbeing during pregnancy.
  • Supplementation guidanceConcrete suggestions to discuss with your clinician — never a prescription.

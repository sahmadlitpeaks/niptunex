Clinician centre

Clinical overview for healthcare professionals

Specifications, validation summary, sample logistics and reporting examples — written for obstetricians, MFM specialists, and genetic counsellors.

Indication

Singleton pregnancies from 8+0 weeks gestation. Validated for natural conception, IVF, and donor-egg pregnancies.

Sample

10 ml maternal whole blood in a Streck cell-free DNA BCT tube. Stable up to 7 days at room temperature.

Turnaround

5–7 business days from sample receipt to report delivery via secure clinician portal.

What the report contains

Three integrated sections, one structured PDF

Section 1 · Fetal cfDNA

Fetal screening

  • Trisomy 21 / 18 / 13 risk classification
  • Fetal fraction reported (target ≥ 4%)
  • Fetal sex (optional, opt-in)
  • Sex chromosome aneuploidy panel (optional)
  • QC metrics & library statistics
Section 2 · Maternal genetics

Maternal predispositions

  • Polygenic risk scores: GDM, preeclampsia, GH
  • Thrombophilia variants (FV Leiden, prothrombin)
  • Recurrent pregnancy loss markers
  • Suggested monitoring protocols
  • Counselling-ready summary
Section 3 · Nutrigenomics

Micronutrient profile

  • Iron absorption (HFE, TMPRSS6)
  • Folate metabolism (MTHFR, MTRR)
  • B12, vitamin C, vitamin D variants
  • Magnesium & manganese transport
  • Personalised supplementation guidance

Clinical positioning

Where NIPTune X fits in the antenatal pathway

Designed to complement first-trimester combined screening — adding maternal and nutrigenomic dimensions that traditional NIPT does not provide.

  • Average-risk pregnanciesAs an alternative to combined first-trimester screening with greater sensitivity for the targeted trisomies.
  • High-risk pregnanciesMaternal age ≥ 35, prior aneuploidy, or abnormal first-trimester result — adds maternal predisposition data for tailored monitoring.
  • Recurrent pregnancy lossIncludes thrombophilia and miscarriage-associated variant analysis to inform anticoagulation decisions.
  • Patients seeking a personalised approachThe nutrigenomics layer supports evidence-informed prenatal supplementation.

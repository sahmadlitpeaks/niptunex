Frequently asked questions

Everything you might want to know before, during, and after the test — for parents and physicians.

For expecting parents

NIPTune X can be performed from 8 weeks of pregnancy onwards. There is no upper limit during pregnancy.

Yes. The test only requires a small blood sample from your arm — there is no contact with the baby and no risk of miscarriage, unlike invasive diagnostic tests such as amniocentesis or CVS.

For the most common chromosome conditions, NIPTune X has a sensitivity of over 99% with a very low false-positive rate. However, it remains a screening test — a high-risk result should always be confirmed with a diagnostic procedure recommended by your clinician.

Results are typically available within 5–7 business days from the date the laboratory receives your sample. Your clinician will then schedule a consultation to review them with you.

For clinicians

10 ml of maternal whole blood collected in a Streck cell-free DNA BCT tube. Stable for up to 7 days at room temperature, eliminating cold-chain logistics for most regions.

The fetal cfDNA component is validated for natural conception, IVF, and donor-egg singleton pregnancies. A twin-pregnancy variant of the panel is available — please contact medical affairs for the validation summary.

The no-call rate is typically below 2%, most commonly due to low fetal fraction. We offer a complimentary re-draw, generally two weeks later, with no additional charge.

It is designed as a complement to standard antenatal monitoring — informing the frequency of blood pressure checks, glucose tolerance testing, or thrombo-prophylaxis discussions. It is not a substitute for clinical judgement or diagnostic workup.
